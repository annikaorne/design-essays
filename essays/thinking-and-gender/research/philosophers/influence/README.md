# Contemporary influence — synthesis

> Companion research to the philosophers sexism files (`../01-rousseau.md` through `../08-weininger.md`).
> These 10 files document how each thinker shapes today's world across four domains: academic/intellectual, legal/institutional, clinical/scientific, and popular/political.
> Total: ~19,000 words across 10 files, ~230 citations verified in spot-check.

## The case this set is building

The companion set in `../` documents that each of the 8 thinkers believed their sexism and integrated it into their systematic philosophy. These influence files document the other half: the systems these thinkers built are still actively at work in modern institutions, academic disciplines, clinical practice, and popular culture. Together the two sets support the author's claim that "cherry-picking what we like from these thinkers is dishonest because what they built is still operating."

## The headline finding

**The gendered content of these philosophies travels more visibly in popular reception than in academic reception.** Academic disciplines that inherit from these thinkers (Kantian ethics, recognition politics, psychodynamic therapy) generally quarantine the offending material and reconstruct around it. Popular reception does not. The manosphere's use of Nietzsche and Schopenhauer, Peterson's Nietzsche-Jung-Freud synthesis, and Weininger's incel afterlife carry the gendered content forward largely intact.

This has an implication: the academic reconstruction project, whatever its internal successes, does not control the downstream social meaning of these thinkers. The popular carrier of each philosopher is often a gendered carrier.

## The per-philosopher picture

**Rousseau** is alive in three main contemporary registers. Rawlsian liberalism owes him a central debt; Rawls cites the *Social Contract* as foundational for his theory of justice, and the Cohen (2010) and Neuhouser (2008) reconstructions take him seriously as a live political theorist. Civic republicanism (Pettit 1997, 2012; Skinner 1998) is the second register, with participatory-budgeting and deliberative-democracy practice (Porto Alegre, Irish Citizens' Assemblies) invoking his legitimacy framework. Third: progressive education from Pestalozzi and Froebel through Montessori and Reggio Emilia carries Rousseau's pedagogy, often with Sophie's gendered template intact (Jane Roland Martin *Reclaiming a Conversation*, 1985). Contemporary populism's "general will" rhetoric (Müller 2016; Tamir 2019 on nationalism) is the live political legacy.

**Kant** is the most pervasive of the eight. The Kantian tradition dominates contemporary analytic ethics (Korsgaard, O'Neill, Wood, Herman, Schapiro, Hay), Rawlsian liberalism (*Theory of Justice* part III is explicitly Kantian), bioethics (Beauchamp and Childress's *Principles of Biomedical Ethics* uses Kantian autonomy as scaffolding), human-rights law (Universal Declaration, Grundgesetz Art. 1, the German Constitutional Court's *Objektformel* jurisprudence), and cosmopolitan international theory (Benhabib 2004, 2006, 2011). The feminist critique of Kantian autonomy as implicitly masculine (Kittay 1999; Miller 2012) is live and growing. Carol Hay's *Kantianism, Liberalism, and Feminism* (2013) is the most ambitious neo-Kantian feminist reconstruction.

**Hegel** has had a striking revival. Recognition theory (Honneth 1995, 2014; Taylor 1992; Fraser & Honneth 2003) is perhaps the most important contemporary strand: it has entered identity-politics debates directly. Analytic Hegel (Pippin 2008; Brandom 2019; Pinkard 2012) has made him respectable in departments that once ignored him. Žižek's Hegelianism (*Less Than Nothing*, 2012) reaches public audiences. Susan Buck-Morss's *Hegel, Haiti, and Universal History* (2009) reframed his standing for postcolonial discussions. Fukuyama (1992) re-litigated perpetually. The §166 division survives in civil-society theorizing (Cohen and Arato 1992).

**Schopenhauer** has a small but growing contemporary footprint. Serious scholarly work (Janaway, Young, Cartwright, Wicks, Jacquette, Reginster, Shapshay's 2019 ethics reconstruction) coexists with broader pop-cultural uptake: Eugene Thacker's *In the Dust of This Planet* (2011) seeded a cosmic-pessimist subculture that fed *True Detective*; David Benatar's *Better Never to Have Been* (2006) built Schopenhauer-inflected antinatalism into a serious philosophical movement. Wagner reception (Magee; Berry 2006; Young's *The Philosophies of Richard Wagner*, 2014) carries Schopenhauerian gender coding into opera staging. The manosphere circulates "On Women" as a portable misogynist text (Zuckerberg 2018; Nagle 2017).

**Nietzsche** is the contemporary heavyweight across registers. Academic: Deleuze, Foucault, Derrida in continental; Leiter, Clark, Nehamas, Richardson, Reginster in analytic. Popular: Jordan Peterson's Nietzsche-Jung synthesis (*12 Rules for Life*, 2018) is the largest single popular carrier. Robert Greene's self-help. Alt-right: Ronald Beiner's *Dangerous Minds* (2018) traces the Nietzschean right (Spencer, Yarvin/Moldbug, Dugin). The "two Nietzsches" problem — progressive readings (Foucault, Butler) vs. reactionary readings (Dugin, Spencer) — is live. The strongest critiques of the reactionary lineage are Domenico Losurdo's *Nietzsche, the Aristocratic Rebel* (Brill, 2020) and Malcolm Bull's *Anti-Nietzsche* (2011).

**Freud**'s contemporary status is the most bifurcated of the eight. In academic psychology he has been substantially discredited (Grünbaum 1984; Crews 2017), with Mark Solms's neuropsychoanalysis (*The Hidden Spring*, 2021) the most serious rehabilitation attempt. In clinical practice he persists: Jonathan Shedler's 2010 *American Psychologist* paper showed psychodynamic therapy's effect sizes match or exceed CBT for many conditions; Leichsenring and Rabung's 2008 *JAMA* meta-analysis confirmed durability; the APA's Division 39 is psychoanalytic; object relations and relational/intersubjective analysis (Kernberg, Kohut, Benjamin, Mitchell, Aron) are alive. In the humanities he is inescapable (Butler, Žižek, Fink). In popular culture his vocabulary is ambient ("Freudian slip," "repression," "projection," "the unconscious"), and Adam Curtis's *The Century of the Self* (BBC 2002) documents the advertising legacy via Bernays. Peterson's Jung-Freud synthesis carries gendered archetypes to a mass audience. The "feminine masochism" trope persists in pop psychology.

**Proudhon** is the most muted of the eight in contemporary English-language academia, more alive in French scholarship (Castleton's critical editions project) and anarchist studies (McKay's *Property Is Theft!*, 2011; Wilbur's ongoing work). Mutualism surfaces in platform cooperativism (Scholz 2016; Schneider 2018). "Property is theft" circulates as slogan. The patriarchal-left critique is a live internal conversation in anarchist circles (McKay, Wilbur), and Karen Offen's 1987 rediscovery of Jenny d'Héricourt has fed feminist historiography of French socialism (Landes 1988; Scott 1988).

**Weininger** is largely a historical-scholarly object today. The anchor scholarship is Sengoopta (2000), Le Rider (1993), and the Hyams-Harrowitz volume (1995). Wittgenstein studies sustain a minor industry (Stern and Szabados 2004; Janik; Monk 1990). Literary modernism studies (Reizbaum on Joyce; Luft on Musil; Robertson on Kafka) keep his name in circulation. In popular culture he survives almost exclusively in manosphere/incel/red-pill spaces as a "based" philosophical ancestor of sex-essentialism (Nagle 2017; Ging 2019). Weininger is the clearest case where the influence is the misogyny; there is nothing else traveling.

## Cross-cutting patterns

1. **The separation between academic and popular inheritance is wide.** Academic philosophy has extensive reconstructive literatures for most of these thinkers. Popular reception does not reconstruct; it appropriates. The two circuits barely intersect.

2. **The manosphere has a curated pantheon.** Nietzsche and Schopenhauer dominate; Freud via Peterson; Weininger in niche incel spaces. Kant, Hegel, Rousseau, and Proudhon are nearly absent. The manosphere chooses philosophers whose gendered content is most readily quotable and least buffered by reconstruction.

3. **The academic reconstruction project succeeds unevenly.** Kant has a thriving reconstruction (Hay, Kleingeld, Denis). Hegel has productive middle positions (Honneth, Fraser, Benhabib). Rousseau has careful partial successes (Weiss 1993; Cohen 2010). Schopenhauer is thinner (Shapshay 2019 is the landmark). Proudhon's reconstruction is happening in anarchist circles rather than academic philosophy. Weininger cannot be reconstructed; nothing is left after the misogyny is extracted.

4. **Gendered content travels with the popular inheritance in every case except (arguably) Proudhon.** Rousseau's pedagogy carries Sophie; Kantian autonomy is critiqued as masculine by care ethics; Hegel's §166 survives in civil-society frameworks; Schopenhauer's "On Women" circulates online; Nietzsche's misogyny is amplified by Peterson and the manosphere; Freud's gendered developmental theory survives in object relations, pop psychology, and Peterson; Weininger's influence just is his misogyny.

5. **Popular intellectual formation runs through a small number of very influential carriers.** Jordan Peterson alone is the largest popular carrier of Nietzsche, Jung, and Freud combined. Ryan Holiday carries (selectively Nietzsche-adjacent) Stoicism. Adam Curtis carries Freud-via-Bernays. A handful of carriers shape the public understanding of these thinkers for millions of readers and viewers.

## The essay-relevant conclusion

If the author's thesis is that the hierarchy of thought that treats men's thinking as serious and women's thinking as frivolous was built by specific philosophers and is still operating, this research supports the "still operating" half in four concrete ways:

1. **Kant and the institutions of liberal modernity.** Bioethics, human rights law, constitutional dignity jurisprudence, Rawlsian political philosophy are Kantian at the core; the feminist critique shows how the gendered foundations survive even inside the nominally universalist frame.

2. **Hegelian recognition as the contemporary political grammar of identity.** The debates over recognition (Honneth vs. Fraser; Taylor on multiculturalism) are live in both philosophy and policy. The gendered §166 division is one of the things being argued about, often implicitly.

3. **Clinical psychoanalysis and Peterson-style pop psychology.** The Freudian developmental framework, challenged in academic psychology, survives in psychodynamic practice and is carried into mass culture with gendered content largely intact.

4. **The manosphere's philosophical curriculum.** Nietzsche, Schopenhauer, and Weininger are live in contemporary misogynist discourse in a way that goes beyond rhetoric into what looks like a canon. This is the clearest case of the specific philosophical content being used today to do specific gendered political work.
